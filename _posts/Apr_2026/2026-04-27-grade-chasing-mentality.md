### Why is grade chasing is overrated?

One reason is that encourages working on things you are already good at. Say you're strong in crimpy, static slabs. Then you'll probably really enjoy working on those types of boulders (and be 1-3 grades stronger than your anti-style). So to "get the next grade", you go out finding something that *perfectly suits your style*. The problem with that, though, is it locks you into working on things you are **already good at**. 

The ignores a whole lot of **other things in the toolbelt** you could focus on!

### What grade chasing is good for

I would say that when starting out, grade chasing is nice as a motivation factor. The satisfaction of sending your first V4, V5, V6, etc. is what encourages people to try boulders that are harder, and things that are beyond your comfort level. 

### Climb things because you learn from them, don't focus on the grade itself

At the end of the day, I think boulders that challenge you to step outside your comfort zone and to learn new skills are far more important than caring about the grade itself.

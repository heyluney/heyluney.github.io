### We all like to climb things that we're good at (grade chasing)

Climbing things that we're good at gives us an ego boost. I can declare to the world I am a **v7 boulderer** - conveniently ignoring the fact that this is only for a specific subset of boulders - crimpy slab. I'm probably more a **v4-v5** boulder on overhang or boulders with dynos. I make a lot more progress on that crimpy slab boulder type. I feel a lot more confident on that style. I enjoy climbing boulders of that style more.

### Ego

When climbing things that are your style, you often are able to do things better than other people in your climbing group. You get praise. Adoration. Incredulity. This makes you feel good.

### Comfort zone

When we climb things that aren't our style, we are forcing ourselves outside of our comfort zone. This is uncomfortable, but it's important. Forcing yourself to climb things **despite** being uncomfortable is great mental training for climbing hard things in general. Climbing is as much a mental sport as it is a physical sport.

### Confidence boost

When you climb things that you don't consider yourself to be good at, but you make progress on it anyway, your confidence will grow. This will in turn lead you to have more motivation for tackling that genre of problem (positive feedback loop)!
